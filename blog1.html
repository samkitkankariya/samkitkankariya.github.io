From Hitchhike to Hike

Image: The View 🏔️

There's an interesting story behind this view. I had halted at Dhankar for a night, and I wanted to go to Kaza. But as Dhankar is situated amidst the mountains, the highway from Tabo to Kaza bypasses Dhankar. So you have to hitchhike from Dhankar to the main road, where you catch a bus, okay?

So I left my homestay around noon and stood at the fork in the road which went to Kaza. I waited for half an hour but not a single vehicle stopped. As it was noon, locals had already left for work in the morning so, all vehicles that I stuck out my thumb to were tourist vehicles. So no luck there.

Now this is where it gets interesting. I had read that there is also a trail that leads you to the road from the village (bhaiya from homestay had said that it takes around 40 minutes). While waiting there I just asked casually to a person nearby who worked there in a hotel - Bhaiya chalke jaane ko kitna time lagega?". Now, that bhaiya was over enthusiastic, he said "Aree wo toh pass mein hi hai, 10 min ka toh raasta hai". This made me excited and I was like, "kya hitchhiking, mein toh hike karke jaunga!"

Image: the yoga bar that helped me get through the hike 🍫

I started descending with 9 kgs of luggage, a yoga bar and a water bottle. Oh boy!! It was the longest 10-minute hike I have ever done, so long that it took me 2 hrs to finish it. Along the way, there were pointy rock formations about 10 feet or so above ground. I sat on one of them and just soaked up the beautiful view. I truly felt infinite. That's where I captured this picture.

I was glad, I didn't get that ride and thanks to that optimistic bhaiya! Most of the time, things don't happen as we want them to, but after a while once we start to see the bigger picture it all starts to make sense. The moments where we feel to the fullest appear amidst the cumbersome hike. So get out of your comfort zone, I bet on my life you will have a big smile on your face once you finish that hike. See you on the other side of fear, Peace❤️
