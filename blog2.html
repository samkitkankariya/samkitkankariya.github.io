The Nights - Avicii

Image: Under the Stars ✨

My first daring adventure was a spontaneous one. Two friends with no knowledge of the trail routes of the Rajgad fort, a torch, a bedsheet to sleep on (not even a tent), and pockets full of 'aaj kuch toofani karte hai wala' attitude went on a night trek.

We climbed the fort in complete darkness as the moon was going to rise at 4 am. To be honest, it felt eerie, as even the rustling of leaves felt like there was someone coming from behind. We reached the fort with puffed lungs and growling stomach.

The fort has a Padmavati temple and a pond below, but the actual top requires a 10-15 minute trek and a steep 80* climb. As we reached the temple, we saw people had set up there tents in front of temple and some had slept inside. We had our dinner inside the temple.

Now, this is where it gets interesting. My friend said, 'Let's sleep here, but I wanted to go to the very top. We came out of the temple and started walking towards the top. Just as we pointed our torch into the stark darkness, we saw a pair of golden eyes. That scared the hell out of us. He said, 'Aik samkya, nako jayla, zopu apan ithech, and my reply was, "Kadhi tari maraychach aahe, aaj nahi tar udya.' On we went, half scared, half excited, and full of adrenaline rush.

Once we reached the top, it felt like we had conquered the world, and the universe was celebrating this moment by decorating itself with a sky full of stars. That night we slept on the enormous mattress called Earth, under the starry blanket, watching the beauty of the cosmos unfold itself in the form of shooting stars passing by!! At that moment, it felt like I was watching the universe, but also I was the universe. On that night, we "lived" the song 'Nights' by Avicii, with each and every cell in my body resonating with the universe, singing 'these are the nights that never die...

May this blog be a catalyst, a push that's needed for the daring adventure that's still pending in your bucket list because "One day, you'll leave this world behind. So live a life you will remember."

p.s. To watch the stars in the background, full brightness is recommended on your screen and on your face. :)
